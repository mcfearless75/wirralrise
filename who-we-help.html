Who We Help

Is Wirral Rise Right for Your Child?

We support children aged 4–11 across Wirral and Merseyside. No diagnosis or label is required — if a child is struggling, we want to help.

Who We Help

Children We Work With

Here's a guide to who we can help and how to make a referral.

Children aged 4–11

Primary-age children struggling to access education in a mainstream setting. No diagnosis or EHCP required.

Children with Additional Needs

Autism, ADHD, PDA, sensory processing differences and other neurodevelopmental profiles.

EBSA & Anxiety

Children experiencing EBSA, anxiety or demand avoidance that is preventing regular attendance.

Trauma-Affected Children

Children whose access to education is affected by adverse childhood experiences or trauma.

At Risk of Exclusion

Pupils at risk of permanent exclusion who need a supported, therapeutic alternative placement.

Not sure if we can help? Just ask us

How to Make a School Referral

  1. 1

    Give Us a Call or Email

    Get in touch for a friendly, no-obligation chat to see if Wirral Rise is the right fit.

  2. 2

    Share Relevant Documents

    School reports and any professional assessments help us understand the child's needs. An EHCP is not required.

  3. 3

    Come for a Settling Visit

    We invite the child and family to see our hub, meet the team and get comfortable before any placement starts.

  4. 4

    Agree a Placement

    We work with the school and local authority to agree funding and a start date that works for everyone.

We make the referral process as simple and stress-free as possible.
